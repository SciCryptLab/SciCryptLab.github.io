What each signal measures

  • Traceability (15%). Files are tracked and clean rather than untracked or modified out from under their history.
  • Reproducibility (25%). The history contains [SciTrace:Run] records with inputs and scripts present and outputs not hand-edited. An opt-in deep check re-executes the recorded command and byte-compares the outputs — cached, with auto-invalidation when scripts or inputs change.
  • Manipulation transparency (20%). Undocumented and high-risk data transforms cost points; documented, routine ones don't.
  • AI-text detection (10%). SciVerify scans every IMRaD section for 15 heuristic indicators of LLM authorship. The inverted score (100 − AI-likeness) feeds the integrity formula: the more human the text reads, the higher the contribution.
  • Reference validation (20%). Each reference is resolved against Crossref and scored on five signals — author, title, year, journal, volume. The average across all references feeds the formula. Mismatched or unresolvable DOIs pull the score down.
  • Author verification (10%). Every listed author is checked against OpenAlex for existence, ORCID linkage, and topical field-match. The average across authors feeds the formula. Unverifiable or mismatched authors cost points.
