SciVerify · feature

The data transforms you forgot to mention, surfaced.

A local LLM reads each script and classifies what it does to your data across 11 transformation categories — filtering, scaling, imputation, outlier handling and more — assigning each a risk level. Undocumented or high-risk steps light up orange and red, right in the lifecycle graph.

What it is

Manipulation detection runs your scripts through an Ollama-powered analysis that identifies each data transformation and grades it: none, info (standard, documented), warn (potentially problematic or undocumented), or critical (substantial alteration without justification). Each flag comes with the transformation type, the script path, and a plain-language rationale.

What it can do

  • 11 categories. Filtering, transformation, aggregation, merging, reshaping, cleaning, encoding, sorting, outlier handling, feature creation, statistical computation.
  • Four risk levels. Routine steps stay quiet; the questionable ones are escalated, not buried.
  • Explained, not asserted. Every flag includes the rationale and the exact script it came from.
  • Visible in context. Risk tints appear on the corresponding nodes of the lifecycle graph.
  • Feeds the score. The findings drive SciVerify's manipulation-transparency dimension.
