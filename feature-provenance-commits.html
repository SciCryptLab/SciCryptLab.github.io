SciTrace · feature

Run the script. Get the provenance for free.

Every time you execute a script through SciTrace, the run is written back into history as a [SciTrace:Run] commit — the exact command, its inputs, its outputs and its exit status, captured as machine-readable JSON. Your dataflow grows a structured, trustworthy record without you writing a line of documentation.

What it is

A run record is a normal commit with a structured block embedded in its message, bracketed by --- SciTrace:Record-Begin --- and --- SciTrace:Record-End ---. Because it lives in the repository's history, the provenance is durable, diff-able, and impossible to silently desync from the data it describes.

What it can do

  • Capture the full command. The command, its arguments, the working directory, exit code, timing, and resource usage are all recorded.
  • Tie inputs to outputs. The record names which files went in and which came out — the edges your lineage graph is built from.
  • Power the re-run button. Wherever SciTrace finds a [SciTrace:Run] commit, it offers to re-execute that exact run.
  • Feed the integrity score. SciVerify's reproducibility check searches your history for these records as ground truth.
