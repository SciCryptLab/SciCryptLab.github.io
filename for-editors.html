The problem with the system today

You can find these problems. You just can't find all of them.

Manual reference cross-checking, author identity checks, AI-text flagging, image-duplication review — you can do all of it, and on a quiet week you do. The problem is that the share of submissions that don't deserve a reviewer's evening has grown faster than the reviewer pool. Fabricated DOIs land in submissions every week. AI-generated discussion sections read fluent. Image duplication hides in plain sight. Ghost authors slip through. The work is no longer a corner of the desk; it is the desk.

The result is that triage has eaten the time evaluation is supposed to protect, and a growing share of the journal's most qualified readers spend their hours separating the manuscripts worth a real review from the ones that should never have reached a reviewer at all.

How SciCrypt solves it

Five automated checks per submission. All re-runnable. All explainable.

  • References. Every entry parsed, DOIs resolved against Crossref, scored on five signals (author, title, year, journal, volume). Fabricated DOIs surface immediately.
  • AI text. 15 heuristic indicators across IMRaD sections — pasted chatbot prefixes, tortured phrasing, hallucinated citations, sentence-cadence patterns, topical drift. Critical hits force a section into the "likely AI" band on their own.
  • Authors. Each author checked against OpenAlex for existence, ORCID linkage, and topical overlap with the manuscript. Ghost authors and identity fraud surface.
  • Data integrity. If the author linked a SciTrace dataflow, every figure is scored for traceability and reproducibility, with provenance commits as ground truth. Manipulation transparency is assessed across the full dataset.
  • One integrity number. Six weighted signals — traceability, reproducibility, manipulation, AI detection, references, and authors — combined into a single banded score. The full evidence is one tab click away.

How it shows up in your day.

1

Open the dashboard.

Your queue shows every pending submission with its integrity score, reviewer status, and decision state. Sort by red. Triage from there.

Open one submission.

Five tabs: Submission, Figures, References, AI Text, Authors. Each tab shows full evidence, not a summary. Click a flagged reference to see the Crossref payload. Click a flagged AI passage to see the highlighted text. Click a flagged author to see the OpenAlex profile.

3

Re-run anything stale with one click.

The author resubmitted a revision? Hit Re-run on References, AI Detection, or Authors. Caches invalidate per check. Nothing wasted, nothing missed.
